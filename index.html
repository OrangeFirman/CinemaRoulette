WHY THIS
EXISTS.

The Problem

Every film arrives pre-judged. Ratings, trailers, reviews — by the time you hit play, you already know what to think. Someone calls it a masterpiece and you spend two hours waiting for it to appear. Someone calls it unwatchable and you spend two hours collecting evidence. The film never gets a fair hearing.

Watch blind and something shifts. No rating, no trailer, no context. Just the film and your own uncontaminated reaction. Then find out what the world thought afterwards. You'll disagree more than you expect. That's the point.

The Philosophy

Quentin Tarantino didn't develop his taste by watching great films. He developed it by watching everything, including the bad ones. Working in a video store, he had no algorithm steering him toward quality. He just watched what was there. That indiscriminate exposure is a large part of what made him one of the most distinctive filmmakers alive.

Bad films teach you things good films can't. When something doesn't work, the mechanics are visible. You see the seams. You notice the moment a scene loses tension, or why a character never earns your sympathy. Greatness tends to hide its own construction. Failure puts it on display.

CinemaRoulette isn't built for people who want to watch the best. It's for people who want to actually develop taste: watching outside your comfort zone, sitting with films you wouldn't have chosen, and forming your own opinion before the internet forms it for you.
